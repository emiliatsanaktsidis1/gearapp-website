Gear – Buyer Protection Policy

Last updated: 13 December 2025

Buyer Protection is designed to protect buyers from clear cases of fraud. It is not a general warranty program.

1. What Buyer Protection Covers

You may be eligible for a refund where Gear determines that fraud occurred, including:

  • Non-shipment (no valid tracking or proof of shipment);
  • Fake tracking numbers or clearly invalid tracking;
  • Receiving an item that is completely different from the listing;
  • Receiving a clearly counterfeit item that was listed as genuine.

Disputes and Item Not Received

If a buyer believes an item has not been received or that a transaction is disputed, the buyer must notify Gear as soon as reasonably possible through the App.

Gear may review available information, including:

  • Tracking details and courier confirmations;
  • Listing descriptions and images;
  • Messages between buyer and seller;
  • Account history and risk indicators.

An item is generally considered delivered when valid tracking information indicates delivery or a delivery attempt at the buyer's address or delivery area.

If a seller cannot provide valid proof of shipment or provides fake or invalid tracking, Gear may treat the transaction as non-shipment and may refund the buyer and reverse or withhold the seller's payout.

If valid tracking shows delivery, courier delays, or in-transit status, the issue is generally considered a courier matter and is not eligible for a refund, unless Gear determines fraud has occurred.

Gear's decision on disputes and non-delivery claims is final.

Shipping Timeframe

Sellers must ship items within 5 business days of payment confirmation, unless a longer handling time is clearly stated in the listing.

If a seller fails to ship an item within the required timeframe and cannot provide valid proof of shipment, Gear may treat the transaction as non-shipment and may refund the buyer and reverse or withhold the seller's payout.

2. What Buyer Protection Does Not Cover

  • Fitment or compatibility issues;
  • Change of mind;
  • Minor condition disputes where the listing was generally accurate;
  • Courier delays or courier-caused damage where the seller shipped as described;
  • Non-receipt claims where tracking confirms delivery, unless fraud is proven.

3. How to Make a Claim

Contact Gear through the App and provide order details, evidence (photos/videos), messages, and any courier or tracking information.
