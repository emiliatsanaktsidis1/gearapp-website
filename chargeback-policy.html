Gear – Chargeback Policy

Last updated: 13 December 2025

A chargeback happens when a buyer disputes a card transaction with their bank or card issuer. Stripe manages chargeback workflows on behalf of Gear.

1. What Happens During a Chargeback

  • The bank/card issuer controls the chargeback process and makes the final decision.
  • The disputed amount may be withdrawn from the seller’s Stripe balance while the dispute is active.
  • Stripe may charge a non-refundable dispute fee.

2. Seller Responsibilities

Sellers are responsible for providing evidence to contest a chargeback, which may include:

  • Proof of shipment and delivery (tracking, courier confirmations, delivery scans/photos if available);
  • Photos of the item and packaging prior to shipment (if available);
  • Messages between buyer and seller;
  • A copy of the listing showing the described condition and details.

3. Gear’s Role

Gear may assist with organising evidence but cannot control the bank’s outcome. Gear is not responsible for losses resulting from chargebacks or dispute fees imposed by Stripe or card issuers.
