Gear – Seller Protection Policy

Last updated: 13 December 2025

Seller Protection is intended to protect honest sellers from unfair refund demands and abusive behaviour, while still allowing Gear to act in cases of fraud.

1. When Seller Protection Applies

Sellers are generally protected when they:

  • Accurately describe the item, including condition and known defects;
  • Ship the correct item as listed;
  • Package the item appropriately;
  • Provide valid tracking that shows delivery to the buyer’s address or delivery area.

2. Examples Where Sellers Are Protected

  • A buyer claims non-delivery but tracking confirms delivery;
  • A buyer is unhappy with compatibility or fitment after purchase;
  • A buyer reports courier delay after the item was shipped with valid tracking;
  • A buyer reports courier damage where the seller shipped the correct item as described.

3. When Seller Protection Does Not Apply

  • The seller fails to ship or cannot provide valid proof of shipment;
  • The seller provides fake or clearly invalid tracking;
  • The seller ships a counterfeit item listed as genuine;
  • The seller ships an item that is completely different from the listing;
  • Other fraudulent or misleading conduct.

4. Gear’s Actions

Where Gear determines fraud has occurred, Gear may refund the buyer and reverse or withhold the seller’s payout, and may restrict or suspend the seller’s account.
