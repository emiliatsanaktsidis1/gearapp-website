Shipping & Protection

Last updated: 6 February 2026

This page contains Gear's policies on shipping, buyer protection, and seller protection. These policies are incorporated into and form part of the Terms of Service.

Shipping Policy

Gear is a marketplace platform. We do not physically handle, package, or ship items. Shipping is arranged and completed by sellers and their chosen couriers.

1. Seller Responsibilities

  • Accurately state shipping options, dispatch timeframes, and shipping costs in the listing.
  • Package items safely and securely to reduce damage in transit.
  • Ship within the timeframe stated on the listing (or within a reasonable time if no timeframe is stated).
  • Provide valid tracking information where available.

2. Buyer Responsibilities

  • Provide a complete and accurate delivery address at checkout.
  • Review shipping options and costs before purchasing.
  • Monitor tracking updates and contact the courier where appropriate.

3. Tracking and Delivery

An order is generally considered delivered when courier tracking indicates Delivered or an equivalent status (including delivery attempt at the buyer's address).

If tracking shows delivery to the correct area/address, Gear will not normally treat the order as undelivered unless there is evidence of fraud or a courier-confirmed delivery error.

4. Lost Shipments

If an item is confirmed lost in transit by the shipping carrier:

  • Buyer: Will receive a full refund. Buyers should not pay for items they never received.
  • Seller: The pending payout will not be released. Sellers may lodge a claim with the shipping carrier or their shipping insurance provider to recover the item value.
  • Gear: Facilitates the refund process but does not absorb shipping carrier losses.

4.1 Tracked Shipments (Recommended)

If tracking confirms the parcel is lost:

  • The buyer receives a full refund.
  • The seller's payout is not released (because delivery never occurred).
  • The seller can lodge a claim with the courier (Australia Post, Sendle, etc.) to recover the item value.
  • If the seller purchased shipping insurance, they can claim the full item value from the insurer.

4.2 Untracked Shipments

If the seller shipped without tracking:

  • If the buyer reports non-delivery, the buyer is refunded.
  • The seller's payout is not released.
  • Without tracking, there is no proof of delivery, so the seller absorbs the loss.

We strongly recommend using tracked shipping for all orders.

5. "Delivered" But Not Received

If tracking shows "Delivered" but the buyer claims non-receipt:

  1. Check surroundings: Buyer should check with neighbours, building reception, or safe drop locations.
  2. Wait 48 hours: Sometimes parcels are scanned delivered prematurely.
  3. Contact Gear support: If the item still cannot be located after 48 hours.
  4. High-value items ($200+): Gear may request a statutory declaration or police report before processing a refund.

If tracking confirms delivery to the correct address, the seller is generally protected and the payout will proceed.

6. Damaged Shipments

If an item arrives damaged due to transit:

  • Document immediately: Take photos of the item and packaging before discarding anything.
  • Report to seller: Notify the seller within 48 hours of receipt.
  • Carrier claim: The seller should lodge a damage claim with the shipping carrier.

Gear does not cover damage caused by shipping carriers, but may assist in dispute resolution. Sellers who purchase shipping insurance can claim damages from their insurance provider.

7. Delayed Shipments

Delays caused by couriers, customs, weather, or operational issues are outside Gear's control. Gear does not provide refunds for delayed deliveries.

8. Shipping Recommendations for Sellers

  • Use tracked shipping: Protects you with proof of delivery and allows you to claim from the courier if lost.
  • Consider shipping insurance: For high-value items, insurance allows you to recover the full item value if lost or damaged.
  • Package securely: Use appropriate packaging materials to prevent damage in transit.
  • Keep receipts: Retain shipping receipts and proof of postage for your records.

Buyer Protection Policy

Buyer Protection is designed to protect buyers from clear cases of fraud. It is not a general warranty program.

1. What Buyer Protection Covers

You may be eligible for a refund where Gear determines that fraud occurred, including:

  • Non-shipment (no valid tracking or proof of shipment);
  • Fake tracking numbers or clearly invalid tracking;
  • Receiving an item that is completely different from the listing;
  • Receiving a clearly counterfeit item that was listed as genuine.

2. Disputes and Item Not Received

If a buyer believes an item has not been received or that a transaction is disputed, the buyer must notify Gear as soon as reasonably possible through the App.

Gear may review available information, including:

  • Tracking details and courier confirmations;
  • Listing descriptions and images;
  • Messages between buyer and seller;
  • Account history and risk indicators.

An item is generally considered delivered when valid tracking information indicates delivery or a delivery attempt at the buyer's address or delivery area.

If a seller cannot provide valid proof of shipment or provides fake or invalid tracking, Gear may treat the transaction as non-shipment and may refund the buyer and reverse or withhold the seller's payout.

If valid tracking shows delivery, courier delays, or in-transit status, the issue is generally considered a courier matter and is not eligible for a refund, unless Gear determines fraud has occurred.

Gear's decision on disputes and non-delivery claims is final.

3. Lost Parcels

If your parcel is confirmed lost in transit by the shipping carrier:

  • You will receive a full refund. Buyers should not pay for items they never received.
  • The seller's payout will be withheld and the seller can lodge a claim with the shipping carrier.

3.1 "Delivered" But Not Received

If tracking shows "Delivered" but you haven't received your item:

  1. Check your surroundings: Look around your property, check with neighbours, and contact building reception if applicable.
  2. Wait 48 hours: Sometimes parcels are scanned as delivered prematurely.
  3. Contact Gear support: If the item still cannot be located after 48 hours, contact us through the App.

For high-value orders ($200+), Gear may request additional verification (such as a statutory declaration or police report) before processing a refund where tracking confirms delivery.

4. Shipping Timeframe

Sellers must ship items within 5 business days of payment confirmation, unless a longer handling time is clearly stated in the listing.

If a seller fails to ship an item within the required timeframe and cannot provide valid proof of shipment, Gear may treat the transaction as non-shipment and may refund the buyer and reverse or withhold the seller's payout.

5. What Buyer Protection Does Not Cover

  • Fitment or compatibility issues;
  • Change of mind;
  • Minor condition disputes where the listing was generally accurate;
  • Courier delays or courier-caused damage where the seller shipped as described;
  • Non-receipt claims where tracking confirms delivery, unless fraud is proven.

6. How to Make a Claim

Contact Gear through the App and provide order details, evidence (photos/videos), messages, and any courier or tracking information.

Seller Protection Policy

Seller Protection is intended to protect honest sellers from unfair refund demands and abusive behaviour, while still allowing Gear to act in cases of fraud.

1. When Seller Protection Applies

Sellers are generally protected when they:

  • Accurately describe the item, including condition and known defects;
  • Ship the correct item as listed;
  • Package the item appropriately;
  • Provide valid tracking that shows delivery to the buyer's address or delivery area.

2. Examples Where Sellers Are Protected

  • A buyer claims non-delivery but tracking confirms delivery;
  • A buyer is unhappy with compatibility or fitment after purchase;
  • A buyer reports courier delay after the item was shipped with valid tracking;
  • A buyer reports courier damage where the seller shipped the correct item as described.

3. When Seller Protection Does Not Apply

  • The seller fails to ship or cannot provide valid proof of shipment;
  • The seller provides fake or clearly invalid tracking;
  • The seller ships a counterfeit item listed as genuine;
  • The seller ships an item that is completely different from the listing;
  • Other fraudulent or misleading conduct.

4. Lost Parcels

If a parcel is lost in transit:

  • The buyer will be refunded (buyers should not pay for items they never received).
  • The seller's payout will not be released.
  • Sellers using tracked shipping can lodge a claim with the shipping carrier or their insurance provider to recover the item value. This is why we recommend tracked shipping.
  • Sellers using untracked shipping absorb the loss, as there is no proof of shipment or carrier record to claim against.

Gear facilitates the refund process but does not absorb losses caused by shipping carriers. The party at fault (the carrier) is where claims should be directed.

5. Gear's Actions

Where Gear determines fraud has occurred, Gear may refund the buyer and reverse or withhold the seller's payout, and may restrict or suspend the seller's account.
