Gear – Shipping Policy

Last updated: 13 December 2025

Gear is a marketplace platform. We do not physically handle, package, or ship items. Shipping is arranged and completed by sellers and their chosen couriers.

1. Seller Responsibilities

  • Accurately state shipping options, dispatch timeframes, and shipping costs in the listing.
  • Package items safely and securely to reduce damage in transit.
  • Ship within the timeframe stated on the listing (or within a reasonable time if no timeframe is stated).
  • Provide valid tracking information where available.

2. Buyer Responsibilities

  • Provide a complete and accurate delivery address at checkout.
  • Review shipping options and costs before purchasing.
  • Monitor tracking updates and contact the courier where appropriate.

3. Tracking and Delivery

An order is generally considered delivered when courier tracking indicates Delivered or an equivalent status (including delivery attempt at the buyer’s address).

If tracking shows delivery to the correct area/address, Gear will not normally treat the order as undelivered unless there is evidence of fraud or a courier-confirmed delivery error.

4. Lost Shipments

  • If a seller cannot provide valid proof of shipment or valid tracking, Gear may treat the order as not shipped and may refund the buyer and withhold or reverse the seller’s payout.
  • If valid tracking shows the item is in transit, delayed, or held, this is a courier issue. Buyers should contact the courier directly using the tracking details.

5. Damaged Shipments

If an item arrives damaged, buyers should document the damage immediately (photos of the item and packaging) and contact the seller and courier as soon as possible.

Gear does not provide refunds purely for courier-caused damage, unless Gear determines there is fraud (for example, the seller shipped an entirely different item).

6. Delayed Shipments

Delays caused by couriers, customs, weather, or operational issues are outside Gear’s control. Gear does not provide refunds for delayed deliveries.

7. Fraud Exception

Where Gear determines that a seller acted fraudulently (for example, fake tracking or no shipment), Gear may refund the buyer and reverse or withhold the seller’s payout.
